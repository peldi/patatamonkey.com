Christmas Holidays Videos

Ciao Italia!

We just got back from Guido's firsts Christmas in Italia, and it was a blast.

I added 103 pictures to the December 2005 gallery on guidojack.com, and here are some videos I took while we were there.

  • Christmas Eve: hanging out in Uggiate with the family.
  • Christmas Day: this year we only had a small subset of the family over: 30 people (you think I'm kidding). Here's The Guids on his bis-nonna (great-granma) Enrica.
  • Sock-Drawer-Crib: sometimes I wonder why we even bought a crib at home. We used sock-drawers padded with a folded-up bath towel wherever we went in Italy and they worked great! Look how happy GJ is in this one.
  • Views from La Sperduta one, two and three: my dad's new house on the countryside is absolutely stunning. These are the views from it in the winter (imagine what they're like in the other seasons, with the fields full of sunflowers and wheat. I'll take more videos this summer when we go back.
  • Risotto Contest: the Milan-side of my family takes great pride in their Christmas risotto (and if you tasted it you'd know why). This year we had a contest between Antonio, using local saffron, and Alessandro, using saffron imported from Iran. Unfortunately we couldn't tell the difference once the food was on the table (the feeding frenzy got the best of us). Either way, they were delicious. The joke Alessandro says in the video is this: he pulls out the very special Iranian saffron, shows the illegible writing on it and says "it probably says Esselunga Teheran" ("Esselunga" is a Safeway/Albertsons/Vons-like food chain in Italy).
  • On Nonno Guido: Guido napping on the granpa that he's named after.
  • Happy Napper: I didn't manage to film all the many smiles he made in his sleep (you can only see one at the very beginning), but trust me when I say that both Guido Jack and my sister were VERY happy together! ;)

Another lesson learned is that a 2-months-old baby is GREAT to travel with. We had the little bassinette on the long stretches, and GJ was small enough to fit on the tray-table on the Frankfurt-Bologna flight (see these pictures for other unusual napping places). What I also learned is that toddlers (maybe ages 1 to 3) are HORRIBLE TRAVELLERS. We had screaming devils around us on the plane both ways, and while The Guids was un-phased (slept through it all), all the other travellers in our sections wanted to throw those screaming, wining, restless beasts and their parents off the plane. I don't think I'll do any flying with the baby for those 2-3 years...or I'll buy 50 pairs of ear-plugs and hand them out to people if I do. ;)

November 19, 2005

Pacifier Video

Here's a new little video of our little one: Guido vs. Pacifier

Now before half of you post horrified comments about how pacifiers are the root of all evil, let me give you our perspective:

- first of all, chill out
- nipple confusion: the little one has been exclusively sucking on M.'s boobs for the first month of his life, and loving it. He knows what it's like and is in no danger of forgetting it at this point.
- pacifier addiction: I fully understand that babies don't cry because they are trying to tell you "I want a pacifier!"...it's usually some other reason. So giving them a pacifier doesn't fix the cause of their crying... So our strategy is to give him a pacifier only when he's hungry (while the boob gets ready or the stored breastmilk gets warmed up to room temperature) OR when he's cranky because he's tired, needs pacifying and he can't find his hand.
- because of how spoiled our baby is with Mariah's nipples, he won't take the bottle easily yet. But he'll suck on the pacifier. So when I want him to suck on the bottle I give him the pacifier, let him suck until he spits it out or it falls out, and quickly replace it with the bottle. His mouth is already in 'sucking mode' and he'll suck on the bottle happily (ok, maybe for just a few minutes, then I have to do the same trick again).
- again, chill out, it's fine. Better to pacify on a piece of silicone than on mom's boobs, right moms? ;)
